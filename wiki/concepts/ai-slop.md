# AI Slop & Accidental Anonymity

The failure mode where **fully AI-generated output erases the signal it was meant to
convey** — quality, judgment, or, in self-presentation, the person themselves.

## Accidental anonymity (Tom MacWright)

[[entities/tom-macwright]], quoted by [[entities/simon-willison]], describes job
applications "clearly cowritten by an LLM," linking to an LLM-generated portfolio, which
links to LLM-generated GitHub projects with LLM-generated commit messages
[2026-06-24-quoting-tom-macwright]. His reaction:

> I *don't know anything about these people*. They haven't put themselves out there. They
> haven't said anything true. … The perfected, generated, prompted resume is generic and
> impersonal. It tells me nothing about this person, other than that they use particular
> tools [2026-06-24-quoting-tom-macwright].

The irony is in the title — "Accidental anonymity": optimizing every surface with AI
makes the applicant **indistinguishable and unknowable**, defeating the purpose of an
application [2026-06-24-quoting-tom-macwright].

## Slop vs. leverage

[[entities/andrej-karpathy]] frames the same tension from the producer side: the goal is
to "use agents as **leverage** rather than produce **slop**"
[2026-06-24-sequoia-ascent-2026-summary]. The discipline that avoids slop is
[[concepts/agentic-engineering]]; the deeper reason it can't be fully automated away is
that [[concepts/outsourcing-thinking-vs-understanding|you can outsource thinking but not
understanding]] — and authenticity is a form of understanding made visible.
