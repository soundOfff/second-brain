# The LLM GUI

The idea that LLMs should communicate with people in **their favored visual/spatial
formats** — images, infographics, slides, whiteboards, animations, web apps — rather than
only in text [2026-06-24-2025-llm-year-in-review]. [[entities/andrej-karpathy]] argues
LLMs are the next major computing paradigm (like 1970s–80s computers), so they will
recapitulate familiar innovations — including a graphical interface
[2026-06-24-2025-llm-year-in-review].

## Why text is not enough

"Chatting" with an LLM is "a bit like issuing commands to a computer console in the
1980s." Text is the raw/favored representation for computers and LLMs, but **people
dislike reading text** — it is slow and effortful — and prefer to consume information
visually and spatially, which is why the GUI was invented
[2026-06-24-2025-llm-year-in-review]. Emoji and Markdown are early, primitive ways of
"dressing up" text for easier consumption [2026-06-24-2025-llm-year-in-review].

## Nano Banana as a first hint

Google Gemini's "Nano Banana" image model ([[entities/google-gemini]]) is "one of the
most incredible, paradigm-shifting models of 2025" and an early hint of what an LLM GUI
might look like. Its power isn't image generation alone but the **joint capability of
text generation, image generation, and world knowledge tangled in one model's weights**
[2026-06-24-2025-llm-year-in-review]. The MenuGen "render dishes onto the menu photo"
example is the same capability in action ([[concepts/software-3-0]])
[2026-06-24-sequoia-ascent-2026-summary].

## Extrapolation: neural computers

Pushed further: imagine a device that takes raw video/audio into a neural net and uses
diffusion to **render a UI unique to that moment**. Early computing could have gone the
"neural net" or the "calculator" way and chose the calculator path; one can imagine a
flip where the **neural net becomes the host process and CPUs become coprocessors**, with
deterministic tools as a "historical appendage" [2026-06-24-sequoia-ascent-2026-summary].
