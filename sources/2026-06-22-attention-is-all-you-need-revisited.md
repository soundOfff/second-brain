A retrospective on the transformer architecture eight years on. The piece argues that
the original scaling intuitions held, but that the community underestimated how much of
the gain would come from data curation rather than architecture.

It walks through three surprises: (1) attention sparsity patterns that emerged without
being trained for, (2) the stubbornness of positional encoding choices, and (3) how
little the FFN width mattered past a threshold.
